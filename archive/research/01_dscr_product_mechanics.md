## 1. PRODUCT MECHANICS

### What a DSCR Loan Is

A DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) loan is a **non-QM (non-qualified mortgage)** investment property loan that underwrites to the **cash flow of the subject property** rather than the borrower's personal income. These loans are originated outside of the Fannie Mae/Freddie Mac agency framework and are typically securitized in private-label RMBS or held on lender balance sheets.

### The DSCR Formula

```
DSCR = Gross Rental Income / Total Debt Service (PITIA)
```

Where:

- **Numerator — Gross Rental Income**: Determined by the lesser of:
  - Actual lease/rental income (supported by executed lease agreements), OR
  - Market rent as determined by a **1007 Rent Schedule** (Form 1007 for SFR) or **1025 Small Residential Income Property Appraisal** (for 2-4 units), completed by the appraiser as part of the property appraisal.
  - For short-term rentals (STR/Airbnb): Many lenders use a **12-month trailing income** from platforms (AirDNA, Airbnb/VRBO statements) or a specialized STR appraisal report. Some lenders apply a **haircut of 10-25%** to STR income to account for vacancy and seasonality.

- **Denominator — Total Debt Service (PITIA)**:
  - **P** = Principal payment (on the subject mortgage)
  - **I** = Interest payment (on the subject mortgage)
  - **T** = Property Taxes (annual, divided by 12)
  - **I** = Property Insurance (hazard, flood if applicable, annual divided by 12)
  - **A** = HOA/Association Dues (if applicable, monthly)

### Calculation Example

| Component | Monthly Amount |
|-----------|---------------|
| Market Rent (per 1007) | $2,800 |
| Principal & Interest | $1,650 |
| Property Taxes | $350 |
| Insurance | $125 |
| HOA | $75 |
| **Total PITIA** | **$2,200** |
| **DSCR** | **2,800 / 2,200 = 1.27x** |

### Key Mechanical Notes

- DSCR is calculated on the **subject property only** — the borrower's other rental properties, W-2 income, business income, etc. are **not** part of the ratio calculation.
- The loan qualifies or disqualifies based solely on whether the property's income can service its own debt.
- No personal income verification is performed. No tax returns, W-2s, pay stubs, or profit-and-loss statements are required.
- No Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio is calculated.

## 2. UNDERWRITING CRITERIA

### DSCR Ratio Thresholds and Corresponding Pricing

| DSCR Ratio | Classification | Typical Pricing Adjustment | Notes |
|------------|---------------|---------------------------|-------|
| **>= 1.50x** | Strong coverage | Best pricing (par or near-par) | Premium tier |
| **1.25x - 1.49x** | Adequate coverage | Standard pricing | Most common sweet spot |
| **1.00x - 1.24x** | Breakeven/marginal | +25-75 bps over standard | Property covers its debt but thin margin |
| **0.75x - 0.99x** | Negative cash flow | +75-150 bps; lower LTV caps (65-70%) | Not all lenders offer; borrower must prove reserves and strong credit |
| **< 0.75x** | Deep negative cash flow | Typically declined | Very few lenders will entertain |
| **No Ratio / 0.00x** | No income verification on property | +100-200 bps; LTV capped at 65-70% | Niche product; vacant properties or properties under renovation |

### Loan-to-Value (LTV) Limits

| Scenario | Maximum LTV |
|----------|-------------|
| Purchase, DSCR >= 1.25x, 740+ FICO | 80% (some lenders offer up to 85%) |
| Purchase, DSCR >= 1.00x, 700+ FICO | 75-80% |
| Purchase, DSCR < 1.00x | 65-75% |
| Rate/Term Refinance | 75-80% |
| Cash-Out Refinance | 70-75% (some up to 80% with strong DSCR) |
| Cash-Out Refinance, DSCR < 1.00x | 65-70% |

### Credit Score Requirements

| FICO Range | Availability |
|------------|-------------|
| **740+** | Best pricing, maximum LTV, full product access |
| **720-739** | Slight pricing adjustment (+12.5-25 bps) |
| **700-719** | Standard pricing hit (+25-50 bps); some LTV restrictions |
| **680-699** | Wider pricing adjustments; LTV typically capped at 75% |
| **660-679** | Limited lender availability; LTV 70% max |
| **620-659** | Very few lenders; LTV 65% max; significantly higher rates |
| **Below 620** | Generally unavailable |

Most lenders have a **minimum FICO of 660-680** as a hard floor. The market median minimum is approximately 680.

### Reserve Requirements

| Scenario | Reserve Requirement |
|----------|-------------------|
| 1-4 properties financed | 6 months PITIA |
| 5-10 properties financed | 6-9 months PITIA |
| 11-20 properties financed | 9-12 months PITIA |
| 20+ properties financed | 12+ months PITIA |
| DSCR < 1.00x | Additional 6-12 months often required |
| Cash-out refinance | Proceeds can sometimes count toward reserves |

**Acceptable reserve sources**: Checking/savings accounts, investment/brokerage accounts (typically valued at 70-80% of market value), retirement accounts (valued at 60-70%), vested stock options, other real estate equity (case-by-case).

### Seasoning Requirements

- **Title seasoning**: Most lenders require 6 months minimum before cash-out refinance
- **Flip seasoning**: 6-12 months of ownership before refinancing (delayed financing exceptions exist)
- **Credit event seasoning**: Bankruptcy 3-4yr, Foreclosure 3-7yr, Short sale 2-4yr, Forbearance 12mo
- **Rental income seasoning**: Some lenders require 3-12 months; others accept market rent appraisal for vacant properties

## 3. LOAN TERMS

### Rate Structures

| Product | Description | Typical Rate Premium over 30yr Fixed |
|---------|-------------|--------------------------------------|
| **30-Year Fixed** | Fully amortizing, fixed rate for 30 years | Baseline |
| **40-Year Fixed** | 40-year amortization, fixed rate; lower payment | +0-25 bps |
| **30-Year Fixed with 5yr IO** | Interest-only for first 60 months, then amortizing | +0-25 bps |
| **40-Year Fixed with 10yr IO** | Interest-only for 120 months, then 30-year amortization | +12.5-50 bps |
| **5/6 ARM** | Fixed for 5 years, adjusts every 6 months (SOFR-indexed) | -25 to -75 bps vs fixed |
| **7/6 ARM** | Fixed for 7 years, adjusts every 6 months | -12.5 to -50 bps vs fixed |

### Rate Environment (Early 2025 Data — Verify for Current)

| Product | Approximate Rate Range |
|---------|----------------------|
| 30-Year Fixed, >= 1.25x DSCR, 740+ FICO, 75% LTV | 7.00% - 8.00% |
| 30-Year Fixed, typical borrower profile | 7.50% - 8.75% |
| 5/6 ARM, strong profile | 6.50% - 7.50% |
| Sub-1.0 DSCR, weaker profile | 8.50% - 10.00%+ |

**Spread over conventional**: DSCR loans typically carry a **150-300+ bps premium** over comparable conventional investment property rates.

### Prepayment Penalties (Standard — Supports Securitization Economics)

| Structure | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **5-4-3-2-1** | 5% of UPB in year 1, declining 1% per year (best rate) |
| **4-3-2-1** | 4% year 1, declining 1% |
| **3-2-1** | 3% year 1, declining 1% |
| **2-1** | 2% year 1, 1% year 2 |
| **None** | No prepay penalty (+50-125 bps rate premium) |

### Loan Amounts

| Parameter | Typical Range |
|-----------|--------------|
| Minimum loan amount | $75,000 - $150,000 |
| Maximum loan amount | $2,000,000 - $5,000,000 (some to $10M+) |
| Sweet spot | $150,000 - $1,500,000 |

## 4. ELIGIBLE PROPERTY TYPES

| Property Type | Eligibility | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|-------|
| **Single-Family Residence (SFR)** | Universally eligible | Core product type |
| **Townhomes** | Universally eligible | Including PUDs |
| **Condominiums (warrantable)** | Widely eligible | Must meet condo review requirements |
| **Non-warrantable condos** | Selectively eligible | LTV haircut of 5-10% |
| **2-4 Unit** | Widely eligible | Rental income from all units counted |
| **5-8 Unit** | Selectively eligible | Crosses into commercial territory |
| **Short-Term Rentals** | Increasingly accepted | Higher scrutiny, 5% LTV reduction typical |
| **Mixed-Use** | Limited but growing | Must be 51%+ residential |
| **Condotels** | Limited | Very few lenders; max 65-70% LTV |

**Generally Ineligible**: Raw land, manufactured homes on leased land, commercial-only, agricultural, co-ops, properties in severe disrepair

## 6. DSCR vs. CONVENTIONAL — KEY DIFFERENCES

| Dimension | Conventional (Fannie/Freddie) | DSCR (Non-QM) |
|-----------|------------------------------|----------------|
| **Income Verification** | Full W-2s, tax returns required | **None** — property cash flow only |
| **DTI Calculation** | Required; max 43-50% | **Not calculated** |
| **Property Limit** | 10 financed properties max | **No limit** |
| **Entity Vesting (LLC)** | Not permitted at origination | **Permitted and encouraged** |
| **Loan Limits** | Conforming limits ($766,550) | **No limit** — to $5M+ |
| **Occupancy** | Owner-occ, second home, or investment | **Investment property only** |
| **Prepayment Penalty** | None (prohibited on QM) | **Standard** (3-5 year) |
| **Rate** | Lower (agency-backed) | Higher (+150-300 bps) |
| **Closing Speed** | 30-45 days | **21-30 days** (some 14-21) |
| **Regulation** | Heavy (CFPB, QM, ATR) | Business-purpose exempt |

## 7. RISK CONSIDERATIONS

1. **Correlation risk**: Performance highly correlated to rental market conditions
2. **Operational risk**: Relies on borrower's property management ability
3. **Adverse selection**: Absence of income verification creates inherent risk
4. **Interest rate risk**: ARM products face payment shock at adjustment
5. **Concentration risk**: Heavy lending in specific Sun Belt MSAs
6. **Insurance cost escalation**: Rising costs in FL, TX, LA, CA compress DSCR ratios

## APPENDIX: LOAN SIZING FORMULA

```
Max Monthly PITIA = Monthly Rent / Target DSCR
Max P&I = Max PITIA - Taxes - Insurance - HOA
Max Loan = P&I × [(1-(1+r/12)^-n) / (r/12)]
```

**Example** (Dallas SFR):
- Rent: $2,500/mo | Taxes: $450 | Insurance: $150 | Target DSCR: 1.25x | Rate: 7.50% | 30yr
- Max PITIA = $2,000 → Max P&I = $1,400 → **Max Loan ≈ $200,200** (71.5% LTV on $280K value)
