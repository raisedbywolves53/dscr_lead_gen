## Prompt

B2B Pricing Strategy for High-Value, Low-Volume Intelligence Products in Financial Services

I'm building a B2B lead intelligence platform for mortgage loan officers. Each "lead" is not a name and phone number — it's a deep investor dossier with property portfolio analysis, equity estimates, financing intelligence, entity resolution, verified contact data, and AI-generated talking points. A single closed deal from our leads generates $3,000-$12,500 in commission for the loan officer.

I need to research pricing models and strategies for products with these characteristics:
- High value per unit (each lead can generate thousands in revenue for the buyer)
- Low volume delivery (100-750 leads/month, not 10,000)
- Intelligence product, not raw data (comparable to analyst reports, not mailing lists)
- No control over downstream conversion (we can't verify if the buyer closed a deal)
- Trust asymmetry on rev share (buyer has incentive to underreport closings)
- First-mover product with no direct competitor (no existing price anchor)

### Part 1: Analogous Pricing Models

Research how these types of businesses price their products:
- Financial research/intelligence platforms (Bloomberg Terminal, PitchBook, CB Insights, Gartner, IBISWorld) — how do they price access to high-value intelligence?
- Sales intelligence platforms (ZoomInfo, Apollo, Cognism, Lusha, 6sense, Bombora) — how do they price enriched prospect data vs raw contact data?
- Commercial real estate intelligence (CoStar, Reonomy, Crexi, Real Capital Analytics) — how do they price property/deal intelligence for professionals?
- Exclusive lead providers in other industries (legal leads, medical leads, home services leads) — how do exclusive, high-value leads get priced?
- Investment research (Seeking Alpha, Morningstar, S&P Capital IQ) — how is "alpha-generating intelligence" priced?

For each analogue: what's the pricing model (per unit, subscription, tiered, usage-based, outcome-based), what's the price range, and what justifies the price?

### Part 2: Revenue Models That Solve the Trust Problem

Research models that address the "I can't verify if my buyer made money from my product" problem:
- How do affiliate networks handle attribution and fraud?
- How do performance marketing platforms verify conversions they don't control?
- What contract structures exist for rev share with unverifiable outcomes? (clawback clauses, audit rights, minimum guarantees)
- How do data providers like ZoomInfo handle the gap between "data delivered" and "deal closed"?
- How do consulting firms price deliverables when the ROI depends on client execution?
- What does "value-based pricing" look like in practice for B2B intelligence products?

### Part 3: Pilot-to-Subscription Conversion Mechanics

Research how B2B intelligence/SaaS companies convert one-time trials into recurring subscriptions:
- What pilot pricing strategies maximize conversion to subscription? (loss-leader, at-cost, premium pilot)
- What's the optimal pilot duration and scope?
- What contract terms (month-to-month vs annual, auto-renew, minimum commitment) produce highest LTV?
- How do companies create switching costs that make cancellation painful?
- What expansion revenue strategies work (upsell to higher tier, add markets, add services)?

### Part 4: Pricing Psychology for Professional Services Buyers

Research pricing psychology specific to independent professionals and small business owners who buy business tools:
- How do loan officers currently budget for lead generation? (what % of commission goes to lead costs?)
- What price points create "no-brainer" ROI perception? (e.g., "costs less than one commission")
- How does anchoring work when there's no competitive price reference?
- When does "too cheap" signal low quality in B2B?
- How do tiered pricing structures affect buyer psychology and tier selection?

### Part 5: Specific Pricing Frameworks to Consider

Evaluate each of these models for my specific product:
1. Per-dossier pricing ($25-100/lead) with minimum monthly commitment
2. Monthly subscription tiers (250/500/1000 leads at $X/mo)
3. Hybrid: subscription + per-dossier overage
4. Pilot + subscription ladder ($500 pilot then $1,500/mo then $3,000/mo)
5. Rev share with minimum monthly fee (e.g., $500/mo base + 5% of closed commission)
6. Geographic exclusivity premium (pay more to lock out competitors in your county)
7. Freemium with premium intelligence tiers (basic leads free, full dossiers paid)
8. Annual contract with discount vs month-to-month pricing
9. Performance guarantee (refund/credit if X% of contacts aren't valid)
10. "Concierge" tier (full DFY outreach execution bundled with intelligence)

For each: pros, cons, revenue predictability, alignment with buyer psychology, scalability, and fit for a bootstrapped business with no outside funding.
