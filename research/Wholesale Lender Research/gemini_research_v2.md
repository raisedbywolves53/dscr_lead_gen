The synergy between these components allows agents to transform from simple search tools into "digital employees" that can plan work, execute tasks, and communicate with other software agents.Hierarchical Planning and Reasoning StrategiesModern research agents do not treat a user query as a single prompt but as a multi-layered problem to be decomposed. Query planning involves transforming logically intricate questions into structured sequences of executable sub-queries. In the "Agent Swarm" mode introduced by Kimi 2.5, models are trained through reinforcement learning to decide when to launch sub-agents to handle parallel tasks, optimizing both time and compute. Three primary strategies have emerged for this process:Parallel Planning: This strategy rewrites or decomposes the original query into multiple independent sub-questions in a single pass without iterative interaction. It is highly efficient for tasks like broad literature reviews where sub-queries can be processed simultaneously.Sequential Planning: This model arranges sub-queries into a linear order where each subsequent step depends on the outcomes of previous intermediate steps. This is essential for multi-hop reasoning in domains like financial fraud detection or legal discovery.Tree-Based Planning: The most complex strategy, this involves exploring branching decision spaces and selecting paths through pruning, backtracking, or heuristic-guided search. This allows the agent to abandon unproductive research paths and re-allocate resources to more promising leads.A critical advancement in 2026 is the integration of "thinking tokens" or hidden chain-of-thought reasoning, where models like OpenAI's o3 pause to reflect, organize information, and self-correct before generating external output.
