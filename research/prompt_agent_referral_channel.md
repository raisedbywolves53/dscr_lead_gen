## Prompt

Real Estate Agent as DSCR Referral Partner: ICP Deep Dive, Discovery Methods, and Partnership Models

I'm building a B2B platform that provides scored, enriched investor leads (property owners likely to need DSCR financing) to mortgage loan officers. A key growth channel is partnering with real estate agents who work with investor buyers — the agent introduces their investor client to an LO, and our lead data gives the LO a reason to share deal flow back with the agent. I need to deeply understand how to find, qualify, approach, and convert investment-focused real estate agents into referral partners.

### Part 1: How Investor-Focused Agents Operate

- What percentage of active real estate agents primarily serve investor clients vs. owner-occupant buyers?
- How do investor-focused agents typically find their investor clients? (REIA meetups, BiggerPockets, social media, repeat business, property management referrals?)
- What is the typical transaction volume for an agent who specializes in investor buyers? (deals per month/year, average transaction size)
- How do investor agents currently source financing for their clients? Do they have a go-to lender, or do they shop around per deal?
- What role does the agent play in the financing decision? Do investor clients rely on their agent for lender recommendations more or less than owner-occupant buyers?
- How do agents earn on investor transactions differently than residential? (commission structure, repeat business value, property management referrals)

### Part 2: Identifying Investment-Focused Agents

What are the most reliable signals that an agent works with investors? Specifically:

- NAR designations and certifications that correlate with investor focus: CCIM, RSPS, SFR, ABR, SRES, CIPS — which ones actually indicate investor activity vs. just credential collecting?
- MLS data signals: agents who consistently list non-owner-occupied properties, agents with high volume of cash transactions, agents listing properties in investor-heavy zip codes
- Online signals: BiggerPockets profiles, REIA membership directories, Facebook group participation (which groups?), YouTube/TikTok content about investing, "investor-friendly agent" in Google/social profiles
- Brokerage signals: which brokerages are most likely to have investor-focused agents? (Keller Williams investment divisions, EXP Realty investor tracks, RE/MAX commercial, boutique investment-focused brokerages)
- Transaction pattern signals: agents closing multiple transactions for the same LLC buyer, agents with high percentage of non-owner-occupied listings

### Part 3: REIA (Real Estate Investor Association) Ecosystem

- How many active REIA chapters exist in the United States?
- What is the typical membership size and meeting frequency?
- Who attends REIA meetings? (investor mix: beginners vs. experienced, agent attendance, lender attendance, service provider attendance)
- How do vendors/sponsors typically engage with REIAs? (sponsorship tiers, presentation slots, vendor tables, costs)
- Which national REIA organizations exist and what are their membership sizes? (National REIA, local independent chapters)
- For the following states, identify the most active REIA chapters by city: North Carolina, Florida, Ohio, Pennsylvania, Texas, Georgia, Indiana, Missouri

### Part 4: The LO-Agent-Investor Triangle

- How do loan officers and real estate agents currently share deal flow in the investor space?
- What makes an LO-agent partnership sticky vs. transactional?
- What value can an LO offer an agent that creates a durable referral relationship? (pre-approvals, investor education co-hosting, deal analysis, leads)
- How does our product fit into this triangle? If we give the LO scored investor leads with contact data and financing intelligence, and the LO shares those leads with their agent partner, what does that partnership look like operationally?
- Are there existing models of "lead sharing" partnerships between LOs and agents that we can study or emulate?
- What compliance considerations exist for LO-agent referral arrangements? (RESPA, co-marketing agreements, referral fee rules)

### Part 5: Approaching and Converting Agent Partners

- What outreach channels work best for reaching investment-focused agents? (LinkedIn, email, REIA events, brokerage office lunch-and-learns, Facebook groups, BiggerPockets)
- What messaging resonates with agents? What do they care about most? (more investor clients, faster closings, more repeat business, competitive differentiation, education/authority)
- What are agents' biggest objections to new lender partnerships?
- What does a successful "pitch" to an agent look like for a DSCR lead program?
- How long does a typical agent partnership take to develop from first contact to first referral?
- What retention strategies keep agent partners active? (regular lead flow, co-marketing support, deal celebration, exclusive market access)

### Part 6: Agent ICP by Market

For the following markets, describe the agent landscape and identify the best channels to find investor-focused agents:

- Raleigh-Durham, NC
- Charlotte, NC
- Cleveland, OH
- Philadelphia, PA
- Pittsburgh, PA
- Indianapolis, IN
- Atlanta, GA
- Houston, TX

- Jacksonville, FL

- San Antonio, TX

For each market: estimated number of active agents, investor transaction share, dominant brokerages, active REIA chapters, and any market-specific dynamics that affect the LO-agent-investor relationship.
