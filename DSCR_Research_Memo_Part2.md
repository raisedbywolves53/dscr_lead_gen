# 1. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

## Frank Christiano's Position

Frank operates from the **Matt Weaver Team branch at 6800 Broken Sound Pkwy NW, Boca Raton, FL** under CrossCountry Mortgage — America's #1 retail mortgage lender. CCM's DSCR product is branded as the "Signature Expanded Investor Cash Flow" loan.

**Key advantage:** Matt Weaver is the #1 originator in America for most loans closed (2020-2024). This means Frank's DSCR initiative enters an underexploited lane within an already powerful brand.

# 3. LATIN AMERICAN BUYER DEEP-DIVE

## Market Size

Latin American/Caribbean buyers represent 45% of Florida's international buyer volume. Total LatAm purchase volume: approximately **$4.7 billion annually** in Florida. **68% currently pay all-cash** — representing a potential **$475M-$640M+** in convertible DSCR origination volume if even a modest share of cash buyers could be converted to leverage.

## By-Country Breakdown

| Country | FL Volume | Preferred FL Markets | Property Preferences | Purchase Intent |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Colombia** | $925M (surged from $307M) | Doral, Kissimmee, Weston, Orlando | Condos, SFR, pre-construction | 47% rental use |
| **Brazil** | $762M | Kissimmee, Orlando, Miami Beach | Vacation homes, STR condos | 29% primary residence |
| **Argentina** | $500M+ est. | Miami, Brickell, Doral | Investment condos, SFR | 66% rental intent (highest) |
| **Venezuela** | Significant | Weston, Plantation, Doral, Miami | SFR, townhomes | Capital preservation + residency |
| **Mexico** | Top 5 | Miami, Orlando | Mixed | Mixed |
| **Peru** | Growing | Miami, Fort Lauderdale | Condos | Investment + vacation |
| **Chile** | Growing | Miami | Investment condos | Capital preservation |
| **Ecuador** | Growing | Orlando, Miami | Affordable SFR | Rental income |
| **Dominican Republic** | Growing | Miami, Orlando | Mixed | Mixed |

## Cultural Buying Patterns

**How they find properties:** Word-of-mouth referrals are dominant. Real estate expos in home countries (Expo Inmobiliaria Colombia, FERIA DE BIENES RAÍCES). WhatsApp is the primary communication channel — not email or phone calls. Instagram and YouTube are key social media platforms.

**Financing expectations:** Most Latin American mortgage markets require 40-70% down payments, so 75% LTV DSCR feels extremely generous. Many assume U.S. financing is inaccessible to foreigners — education is the key barrier.

**Trust factors:** Personal relationships and referrals matter more than brand. Bilingual communication (Spanish/Portuguese) is essential. Face-to-face meetings or video calls preferred over email. Community reputation and testimonials from fellow nationals carry enormous weight.

## Documentation Specifics

| Item | Typical Availability |
|---|---|
| Passport | Universal |
| ITIN | ~30-40% of Latin American buyers have one |
| Foreign bank statements | Available but may require translation/notarization |
| Credit history | Most countries don't have U.S.-compatible credit bureaus |
| Proof of income | Complex — many are business owners with non-standardized reporting |
| Visa status | Mix of B-1/B-2, E-2, EB-5, no visa (DSCR works for all) |

**FIRPTA consideration:** 15% withholding on gross sale price when foreign nationals sell. Tax treaty benefits may reduce this. Immigration attorneys and international tax advisors are key referral partners.

## Marketing Channels

**Must-have:** WhatsApp Business presence, Spanish/Portuguese-language content, Instagram presence, real estate expos in source countries, partnerships with immigration attorneys and international RE agents.

**Key organizations:** CAMACOL (Latin American Chamber of Commerce), Colombian American Chamber, Brazilian-American Chamber, Florida Association of Realtors international programs.

## Competitive Gap

Current DSCR lenders serving this segment: Waltz (most LatAm-focused), Milo Financial, A&D Mortgage. Significant gaps remain in native-language borrower experience, country-specific underwriting familiarity, WhatsApp-native communication workflows, and integrated immigration pathway support.

## When to Use Which Lender — Decision Framework

| Scenario | First Choice | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Premium borrower** (740+ FICO, 1.25+ DSCR, SFR) | Price all four | Logan Elite may win on rate; theLender price-matches |
| **Higher LTV** (80-85%) | Change or Open Mortgage | Both go to 85% purchase |
| **Sub-1.0 DSCR** (0.75-0.99) | theLender NearNONI | Designed for this; 620 FICO floor |
| **No-ratio / complex docs** | Change Wholesale | CDFI exemption = unique capability |
| **Foreign national** | Change Wholesale | 75% LTV, no US credit, $2.5M |
| **Lower FICO** (620-659) | theLender NearNONI | Only lender confirmed at 620 |
| **Large loan** ($2-3.5M) | theLender or Open Mortgage | Both go to $3.5M |
| **STR / Airbnb** | theLender | 3 income verification methods |
| **Portfolio / blanket** | theLender (theBlanket) | Only lender offering 3-25 property blanket |
| **ADU-heavy property** | Open Mortgage | Allows SFR with multiple ADUs |
| **Manufactured home** | Open Mortgage | Doublewide eligible |
| **Max broker comp** | theLender | Up to 6 points reported |

## Standard Operating Procedure

On any deal above $150K, run through at least theLender and one other lender (Change for complex deals, Logan for strong-DSCR deals, Open Mortgage for unusual property types) to ensure best execution.
