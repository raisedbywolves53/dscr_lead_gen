# Apollo Credit Burn Plan — Multi-Market Prospect Experiment

## Objective

Before Apollo subscription cancels, maximize enrichment of the highest-value
prospects across multiple U.S. markets. Use credits as a structured experiment
to test which markets and buyer segments yield the best contact data and
eventual conversion.

## Part 3: Cohort Definitions & Search Parameters

### Cohort A: Branch Managers at Independent Brokerages (60% of credits)

**Why highest weight:** One sale = multiple LO users. They have budget authority.
They can use DSCR lead intelligence as a recruiting tool to attract producing LOs.

**Apollo Search Parameters:**
- Titles: "Branch Manager", "Producing Branch Manager", "VP Mortgage Lending",
  "Area Manager", "Sales Manager", "Regional Manager", "Managing Director",
  "President" (at small shops), "Owner" (at small shops)
- Company size: 1-50 employees
- Exclude: Wells Fargo, Bank of America, JPMorgan Chase, Chase, Citibank,
  US Bank, PNC, TD Bank, Capital One, Rocket Mortgage, UWM, loanDepot,
  Freedom Mortgage, Mr. Cooper, Pennymac
- Industry: Financial Services, Real Estate
- Location: Target market city + 25 mile radius

**Post-search scoring signals (zero-credit, data join):**
- Company name matches known DSCR wholesale lender client list
- City matches active REIA chapter location
- Title contains "producing" (indicates they still originate, not just manage)

### Cohort B: DSCR-Specialist Individual LOs (25% of credits)

**Why included:** Faster to close, good for first testimonials, some will become
branch managers. Self-identified DSCR specialists are pre-qualified by interest.

**Apollo Search Parameters:**
- Titles: "Loan Officer", "Mortgage Loan Originator", "Mortgage Broker",
  "Senior Loan Officer", "Loan Originator"
- Keywords (in bio/headline): "DSCR", "non-QM", "investor", "investment property",
  "rental property", "bank statement loan", "private lending", "hard money"
- Company size: 1-50 employees
- Same exclusions as Cohort A
- Location: Target market city + 25 mile radius

**Why keywords matter:** A generic "Loan Officer in Cleveland" search burns credits
on conventional LOs who'll never buy. Keywords filter to self-identified specialists.

### Cohort C: Wholesale Lender Account Executives (15% of credits)

**Why this is the force multiplier:** Every wholesale DSCR lender has AEs covering
territories. These AEs know every producing DSCR broker in their region. One AE
relationship = warm intros to dozens of brokers. They're also potential channel
partners — if they recommend your product to their broker network, it's a
distribution channel.

**Apollo Search Parameters:**
- Titles: "Account Executive", "Regional Sales Manager", "Wholesale Account Manager",
  "Business Development Manager", "Territory Manager", "National Sales Manager"
- Companies (from GPT v2 wholesale lender research):
  - Angel Oak Mortgage Solutions
  - Deephaven Mortgage
  - A&D Mortgage
  - Newfi Wholesale
  - Kiavi
  - Visio Lending
  - Easy Street Capital
  - RCN Capital
  - Lima One Capital
  - Civic Financial Services
  - CoreVest / Redwood Trust
  - Toorak Capital Partners
  - Velocity Mortgage Capital
  - Griffin Funding
  - Truss Financial Group
  - Arc Home
  - Acra Lending
  - Carrington Mortgage
  - Change Lending (Sprout Mortgage successor)
  - MiMutual Mortgage

**Note on AE coverage:** AEs typically cover 1-3 states. Search by state, not city.
A Visio AE covering Ohio knows every DSCR broker in Cleveland, Columbus, Cincinnati,
and Toledo.

## Part 4: Experiment Design

### Phase 1: Test Batch (375 credits, ~1-2 hours)

Run 25 results per cohort per market:

| Market | Cohort A (25) | Cohort B (25) | Cohort C (25) | Total |
|--------|--------------|--------------|--------------|-------|
| Cleveland | Branch mgrs | DSCR LOs | OH AEs | 75 |
| Indianapolis | Branch mgrs | DSCR LOs | IN AEs | 75 |
| Pittsburgh | Branch mgrs | DSCR LOs | PA AEs | 75 |
| Kansas City | Branch mgrs | DSCR LOs | MO AEs | 75 |
| Philadelphia | Branch mgrs | DSCR LOs | PA AEs | 75 |

### Metrics to Track Per Cell

| Metric | What It Tells Us |
|--------|-----------------|
| Email fill rate | Can we reach them via cold email? |
| Phone fill rate | Can we call them? |
| LinkedIn fill rate | Can we run LinkedIn outreach (primary channel per GTM)? |
| Title relevance % | Did Apollo return actual DSCR/mortgage people? |
| Company independence % | Are results at indie shops vs big banks that slipped through? |
| Duplicate rate vs existing NC list | Is Apollo returning the same people across markets? |

### Decision Rules After Phase 1

- **Kill:** Any market × cohort cell with <40% email fill rate AND <50% LinkedIn fill rate
- **Double down:** Any cell with >70% email fill rate OR >80% LinkedIn fill rate
- **Reallocate reserve credits** to top 3 performing cells
- **If Cohort C (AEs) outperforms:** Consider reallocating Cohort A/B credits to more AE searches — AEs are a leverage play, not a volume play

### Phase 2: Scale Winners (2,125 credits)

After Phase 1 analysis, run full searches in winning cells:
- Up to 200 results per winning market × cohort combination
- Enrich any Phase 1 results that came back with partial data
- Expand geographic radius for high-performing markets

## Part 5: Post-Search Enrichment (Zero Credit Cost)

After Apollo results come in, tag every prospect using data from the research:

### Tag 1: DSCR Lender Affiliation
Cross-reference prospect's company against the 20+ DSCR wholesale lenders from
GPT v2 research. If their company IS a wholesale lender or is known to originate
DSCR, flag as "DSCR Confirmed."

### Tag 2: REIA Proximity
Tag any prospect in a city with an active REIA chapter (from Gemini v3):
- Cleveland → Great Lakes REIA (3rd Thursday)
- Indianapolis → CIREIA (1st Thursday)
- Pittsburgh → ACRE (1st Tuesday), PREIA (3rd Tuesday)
- Kansas City → MAREI (2nd Tuesday)
- Philadelphia → DIG, Delco Property Investors

These are meetup targets — prospects you can meet in person.

### Tag 3: Market DSCR Intelligence
For each prospect's market, attach the local DSCR math:
- Cleveland: Median $139,900, DSCR 1.35x, 11.3% gross yield
- Indianapolis: Median $230K, 6.8% yield, +7.9pp occupancy gain
- Pittsburgh: Median $250K, DSCR 1.25+, +6.1pp occupancy gain
- Kansas City: Median $240K, 7.5%+ yield, #1 investor buyer share state
- Philadelphia: Median $290K, strong 2-4 unit market

This becomes talking point material for personalized outreach.

### Tag 4: Lender Guidelines Match
For Cohort C (AEs), tag which lender guidelines would best serve investors in
their market. E.g., for a Kiavi AE covering OH:
- "Cleveland investors: median $139,900, DSCR 1.35x — your min 1.0x DSCR
  and $660 FICO threshold covers 80%+ of this market"

## Why These Markets, Not Others

**Why not Houston/Atlanta/Tampa:** Higher competition from existing lead providers,
weaker DSCR math (higher prices compress ratios), and Sun Belt oversupply concerns
(vacancy ~200 bps above national avg). These are Phase 2 markets after proving the
model in Midwest/Mid-Atlantic cash flow markets.

**Why not Raleigh/Charlotte NC:** That's the home market — already building
organically through LinkedIn + meetups + direct outreach. Don't burn Apollo credits
on a market you're working by hand.

**Why Midwest-heavy:** The research consistently shows Midwest markets (Cleveland,
Indy, KC, Pittsburgh) have the best DSCR math, the most active independent broker
channels, and the least competition from existing lead intelligence providers. The
math works at current rates (7-8.5%) in these markets. In Sun Belt markets, DSCR
math only works for STR or below-market acquisitions.
