# EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) mortgage loan market is experiencing explosive growth, with originations surging 52-123% year-over-year and now comprising approximately 30% of all non-QM mortgage volume. January 2025 alone recorded over 4,272 DSCR transactions totaling $2+ billion. The product has transitioned from a niche offering to mainstream financing — evidenced by Rocket Pro launching its first DSCR product in November 2025.

**Florida is the single most attractive market for DSCR lending in the United States.** The state attracts 20% of all international real estate buyers ($10.4 billion in volume), adds 306,000+ net residents annually, has no state income tax, and sits at the intersection of domestic migration and Latin American capital flows.

The research identifies **19 distinct borrower segments** that use or could benefit from DSCR loans, prioritized into three tiers based on market size, DSCR product fit, growth trajectory, and Florida relevance.

**Critical finding:** The four wholesale lenders analyzed (Change Wholesale, Logan Finance, theLender, Open Mortgage) collectively cover virtually the entire DSCR lending box, but each has distinct competitive advantages that should be matched to specific borrower segments for optimal execution.

# DSCR LOAN FUNDAMENTALS

## What Is a DSCR Loan?

A DSCR loan is a mortgage product for real estate investors that qualifies borrowers based on a property's rental income rather than the borrower's personal income. These are classified as **Non-QM (Non-Qualified Mortgage)** products structured as **business-purpose loans** for non-owner-occupied investment properties.

**The DSCR Formula:**

> **DSCR = Gross Monthly Rental Income / Monthly PITIA**

Where PITIA = Principal + Interest + Taxes + Insurance + Association dues

A property generating $2,500/month in rent with $2,000/month in PITIA yields a DSCR of 1.25 — meaning it produces 25% more income than needed to service the debt.

## Why DSCR Loans Exist

Post-2008 regulations (Dodd-Frank, ATR/QM rules) imposed strict income documentation requirements on consumer mortgages but carved out an exemption for **business-purpose investment property loans** under Regulation Z, Section 1026.3. DSCR loans operate in this exempted space, serving borrowers who cannot or prefer not to qualify through traditional income documentation.

## Income Verification Mechanics

**Long-term rentals:** Lenders use the lower of actual lease rent or the appraiser's estimated market rent (Form 1007 rent schedule).

**Short-term rentals (Airbnb/VRBO):** More complex underwriting. Some lenders use long-term market rent as a conservative baseline; others accept documented STR revenue from platform histories or third-party data providers like AirDNA.

## Qualification Criteria

### DSCR Ratio Thresholds

| DSCR Range | Lender Treatment |
|---|---|
| **1.25+** | Premium tier — best rates, lowest reserves, up to 80% LTV |
| **1.10–1.24** | Acceptable to most lenders; slightly conservative terms |
| **1.00–1.09** | Borderline — requires compensating factors (higher credit, larger reserves) |
| **0.75–0.99** | Sub-1.0 programs exist but carry rate premiums and larger down payments |
| **No Ratio** | Programs eliminate DSCR minimum entirely; qualification on property value/equity (max ~70% LTV) |

### Credit Score Impact

| Credit Score | Impact on Terms |
|---|---|
| **740+** | Premium tier — rate buy-downs, reduced points, expedited processing |
| **700–739** | Full 80% LTV access, best rates, flexible reserves |
| **660–699** | 75% LTV, competitive pricing |
| **620–659** | Max LTV 65–70%, higher rates, larger reserves; some lenders won't underwrite |

### LTV / Down Payment

| Scenario | LTV | Down Payment |
|---|---|---|
| **Strong profile** (700+ credit, 1.25+ DSCR) | 80% | 20% |
| **Standard** (660+ credit) | 75% | 25% |
| **Weaker profile** (low credit or sub-1.0 DSCR) | 65–70% | 30–35% |
| **No ratio programs** | 70% max | 30% |

### Reserve Requirements

Lenders require **6–12 months of PITIA** in liquid reserves (standard is ~9 months). Acceptable sources: savings, checking, money market, brokerage accounts.

### Property Types Allowed

Single-family residences (SFR), 2-4 unit multi-family, condos and townhomes, short-term rentals, rural properties, and properties held in LLCs/corporations/trusts.

### Documentation Requirements

**Not required:** W-2s, pay stubs, tax returns, employment verification, DTI calculation.

**Required:** Property appraisal with rent schedule (Form 1007), lease agreement or rent roll, entity documentation (if LLC/trust), basic borrower identification, credit pull, proof of liquid reserves.

## Typical Loan Terms

### Interest Rates (February 2026)

| Metric | Rate |
|---|---|
| **DSCR baseline par rate** | 5.99–6.62% |
| **Typical range (qualified borrowers)** | 6.00–7.50% |
| **Freddie Mac 30-year fixed (comparison)** | 6.01% |
| **Premium over conventional** | 0.4–1.5% |

Rate calculation: 5-Year U.S. Treasury yield + credit spread of 2.65–4.00%, with spread determined by credit score, LTV, DSCR ratio, loan amount, property type, prepayment penalty structure, and investor experience.

DSCR rates peaked at 8.73% in January 2024, declined to 7.76% by February 2025, and have continued dropping into the low-6% range by early 2026.

### Rate Structure Options

**30-Year Fixed Rate:** Most common. Best for long-term hold investors (10+ year horizon).

**Adjustable Rate Mortgages (5/6 ARM, 7/1 ARM, 10/6 ARM):** Lower initial rate during fixed period. Best for investors planning to sell or refinance within the fixed window.

**Interest-Only with 40-Year Term:** First 10 years IO; remaining 30 years fully amortize. Maximizes cash flow and improves DSCR ratio. Rate premium of ~0.25–0.50%.

### Prepayment Penalty Structures

| Structure | Rate Impact |
|---|---|
| **5-4-3-2-1** | Lowest rate |
| **3-2-1** | Moderate rate |
| **3-0-0 or 1-0-0** | Higher rate |
| **No PPP** | Highest rate |

The rate difference between a 5-year PPP and no PPP can be 50–100+ basis points.

### Loan Sizing

Average DSCR loan size climbed from $329,294 to $354,931 between early 2024 and early 2025. Some programs go up to $3.0–3.5 million.

## Regulatory Framework

DSCR loans are wholly exempt from TILA/RESPA, ATR/QM rules, and HPML requirements under the business purpose exemption. This means lenders are not required to verify income, calculate DTI, or comply with consumer-protection frameworks. Borrowers forfeit consumer protections in exchange for streamlined qualification.

# IDEAL CUSTOMER PROFILE (ICP) UNIVERSE

## Overview

The research identifies 19 distinct borrower segments. Each is evaluated on market size, DSCR product fit, growth trajectory, and Florida relevance, then assigned to one of three priority tiers.

## TIER 1 — PRIMARY TARGET SEGMENTS (Highest ROI for lead generation)

### 1. Individual Real Estate Investors (1–10 Properties)

**Who they are:** The largest segment in the market. 9.72 million tax-paying Americans own rental property. 42% own just one unit; 33% own 2-4 units. 87% of investor-owned homes belong to owners with 1-5 properties. 59% of landlords plan to buy property during 2025.

**Why DSCR:** Many have maxed out Fannie Mae's 10-property limit. Self-employed investors show depressed income on tax returns. No DTI calculation means existing portfolio doesn't count against them. Faster closings (10-21 days). LLC ownership available from day one.

**Typical deal:** $150K–$500K per property. 1-3 properties/year. Portfolio value $300K–$3M.

**How to find them:** Real estate investing Facebook groups, BiggerPockets, local REIA meetups, property management software users, public records (investors with 2-10 properties in a county), investor-friendly real estate agents.

**Florida relevance:** HIGH — Concentrated in Sun Belt states, strong FL presence.

---

### 2. Professional/Serial Investors (10+ Properties)

**Who they are:** Full-time investors building portfolios of 10-100+ units through multiple LLCs. Think in terms of portfolio yield, cash-on-cash return, equity velocity. 81% plan to grow portfolios.

**Why DSCR:** No limit on DSCR loans (vs. conventional 10-property cap). Each property underwritten independently. Portfolio growth not constrained by personal DTI. Cash-out refinance fuels continued acquisitions.

**Typical deal:** $200K–$1M+ per property. 3-10+ properties/year. Portfolio value $2M–$20M+.

**How to find them:** BiggerPockets Pro members, mastermind groups, property management companies (10+ unit portfolios), title company data (serial buyers), real estate CPA/attorney networks.

**Florida relevance:** HIGH — FL is a primary market for portfolio builders.

---

### 3. Short-Term Rental (STR) Operators

**Who they are:** Airbnb/VRBO operators running properties as hospitality businesses, generating 2-3x traditional rental income. Ranges from single-property side-hustlers to 10-50+ listing operators.

**Why DSCR:** Traditional lenders ignore or discount STR income. DSCR lenders who understand STR markets use AirDNA data. No W-2/tax return documentation. STR loophole + cost segregation + 100% bonus depreciation creates massive tax benefits.

**Typical deal:** $250K–$800K per property. 1-5 acquisitions/year. Revenue per property: $40K–$120K annually.

**How to find them:** AirDNA/Mashvisor communities, Airbnb/VRBO host forums, STR conferences, property management software for STRs (Guesty, Hostaway), local STR permit registries, YouTube STR influencers.

**Florida relevance:** VERY HIGH — Orlando (3,861 active listings, top 10% earning $7,186+/month), Destin/Panhandle, Keys are major STR markets.

---

### 4. Foreign National Investors

**Who they are:** Non-U.S. citizens purchasing U.S. investment properties. Foreign buyers purchased 78,100 U.S. homes worth $56 billion (Apr 2024–Mar 2025). Florida attracts 20% of all international buyers — $10.4 billion in volume, up 51% YoY.

**Top nationalities in Florida:** Latin America/Caribbean (45% of FL international buyers), Canada (18%, $1.9B volume), Europe (18%). Colombia surged to $925M, Brazil $762M.

**Geographic concentration:** 45% of international purchases in Miami-Fort Lauderdale-West Palm Beach MSA. South Florida: 64% Latin American/Caribbean, 14% European.

**Why DSCR:** One of the only viable financing paths for non-residents. No U.S. credit score, income, or tax returns required. Rates only ~0.5% higher than domestic pricing (6.62% baseline). Accepts foreign passports and international bank statements.

**Typical deal:** $300K–$2M+ per property. 1-3 properties. Average price higher than domestic buyers. 47% pay all-cash (indicating massive opportunity to convert cash buyers to leveraged buyers).

**How to find them:** International real estate brokerages, foreign consulates, international real estate expos, immigration attorneys, Latin American/Canadian/European investor networks, foreign-language real estate media, Florida Association of Realtors international programs.

**Florida relevance:** CRITICAL — Florida is #1 destination for international buyers. This is arguably the highest-value niche.

---

### 5. Self-Employed Borrowers

**Who they are:** Business owners, entrepreneurs, freelancers, gig workers whose tax returns show significantly less income than actual earnings due to legitimate deductions. Florida has one of the highest concentrations of self-employed individuals in the country.

**Why DSCR:** Tax returns don't reflect true income. No W-2s, pay stubs, employment verification needed. Faster than bank statement loans (which require 12-24 months of statements). LLC ownership is natural for business owners.

**Typical deal:** $200K–$700K per property. 1-5 properties/year. Often buying through existing LLCs.

**How to find them:** CPAs and tax preparers, Small Business Development Centers, Chamber of Commerce, business networking groups, franchise owner associations.

**Florida relevance:** VERY HIGH — High concentration of self-employed residents.

---

### 6. BRRRR Strategy Investors (Buy, Rehab, Rent, Refinance, Repeat)

**Who they are:** Investors acquiring distressed properties with short-term hard money, renovating, stabilizing with tenants, then refinancing into long-term DSCR financing. DSCR is the "R" (Refinance) in BRRRR.

**Why DSCR:** Ideal refinance vehicle — qualification based on post-renovation rental income. Cash-out at 75% LTV on new appraised value recaptures renovation capital. No seasoning (with select lenders). Repeatable and scalable.

**Typical deal:** Purchase $80K–$250K (distressed), ARV $150K–$400K. 3-10+ cycles/year for active investors.

**How to find them:** Hard money/bridge loan lender referrals (the acquisition lender becomes the DSCR referral source), BiggerPockets BRRRR forums, local REIA meetups, wholesaler networks, renovation supply companies.

**Florida relevance:** MEDIUM-HIGH — Cape Coral/Fort Myers post-hurricane rebuilding, Jacksonville value-add opportunities.

---

### 7. Corporate Entities (LLCs, S-Corps, Trusts)

**Who they are:** A structural segment that cuts across all other segments. LLCs are the gold standard entity structure for serious investors. DSCR loans close in the name of LLCs, Corporations, Revocable/Irrevocable Trusts, and Partnerships.

**Why DSCR:** One of the only loan products allowing entity borrowing from the outset. Personal asset protection, credit profile separation, DTI insulation, portfolio segmentation, estate planning through trusts.

**How to find them:** Business formation services, Secretary of State new LLC filings, real estate attorneys/CPAs, title companies. Virtually all serious investors use entities — this is the gateway.

**Florida relevance:** HIGH — Entity-based ownership is standard practice in FL investment real estate.

## TIER 2 — SECONDARY TARGET SEGMENTS (Strong opportunity, requires more targeted approach)

### 8. High-Net-Worth Individuals (HNWIs)

$1M+ investable assets. Use real estate for diversification. Value privacy, speed, leverage over lowest rate. DSCR lets them preserve liquidity rather than paying all-cash.

**Florida relevance:** VERY HIGH — Palm Beach, Naples, Miami are HNWI concentrations.

### 9. Multi-Family Investors (2-4 Units)

Target duplexes, triplexes, fourplexes. Multiple rental income streams create naturally strong DSCR ratios.

**Florida relevance:** MEDIUM-HIGH — Strong demand in Jacksonville, Tampa, secondary markets.

### 10. 1031 Exchange Buyers

Under extreme time pressure (45-day identification, 180-day closing). DSCR's 15-30 day closing and no income documentation are critical advantages.

**Florida relevance:** HIGH — Active exchange market in FL.

### 11. Recently Retired / Career Changers

Substantial assets but no current W-2 income. DSCR + asset depletion bridges the gap.

**Florida relevance:** VERY HIGH — Florida is the #1 retirement destination.

### 12. Tax-Strategy Investors

High-income W-2 earners acquiring rental properties for 100% bonus depreciation (permanently restored July 2025) + cost segregation + STR loophole. DSCR financing is the leverage vehicle.

**Florida relevance:** HIGH — Strong overlap with HNWI and STR segments.

### 13. Real Estate Investment Funds/LLCs

Small to mid-size funds pooling investor capital. DSCR eliminates the complexity of pooled investor income documentation.

**Florida relevance:** MEDIUM-HIGH — Active fund formation in FL.

### 14. Section 8 Landlords

Growing segment (social media-educated millennials/Gen Z). May not qualify for conventional investment loans due to DTI.

Experienced CRE investors adding residential. DSCR's longer terms, fixed rates, and no balloon payments are advantages vs. commercial lending.

### 17. Diaspora Investors

First-generation immigrants building U.S. real estate wealth. Underserved by DSCR lenders. Florida is a primary gateway market.

### 18. Digital Nomads / Expats

Remote workers investing in U.S. rental properties. No U.S. employment documentation needed with DSCR.

### 19. Accidental Landlords Converting to Intentional

Became landlords by circumstance; transitioning to intentional investing. DSCR enables refinance and expansion.

## Insurance Crisis — Critical Factor

**This is the single biggest variable affecting DSCR calculations in Florida:**
- Average annual premium: $5,700+ (highest in U.S.)
- Range by county: Sumter $2,064 to Monroe $9,058
- FL accounts for 9% of claims but 79% of insurance lawsuits
- $3+ trillion in insured coastal property
- Signs of stabilization (10 new insurers approved post-reform), but premiums remain elevated
- Properties with wind mitigation features sell at 3-5% premiums and command lower insurance costs

**Strategic implication:** Insurance costs directly reduce DSCR ratios. Inland properties often offer better DSCR math than coastal. Accurate insurance modeling is critical to underwriting.

## Key Strategic Insight: The DSCR Financing Ecosystem

DSCR loans don't exist in isolation — they're the **permanent financing backbone** of a broader investment lifecycle:

1. **FHA → DSCR Pipeline:** Investor house-hacks with 3.5% down FHA, converts to rental, refinances to DSCR
2. **Hard Money → DSCR Pipeline:** Investor buys distressed property with hard money, rehabs, stabilizes, refinances to DSCR (BRRRR)
3. **HELOC → DSCR Pipeline:** Investor taps primary home equity for down payment, gets DSCR mortgage on investment property
4. **Conventional → DSCR Pipeline:** Investor uses conventional for first 4-10 properties, transitions to DSCR once DTI maxed or 10-property limit reached
5. **Cash → DSCR Pipeline:** Foreign national or HNWI buys all-cash, delayed refinances to DSCR to redeploy capital (especially relevant for FL's 43% cash-buyer market)

# KEY FINDINGS & STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS

## Finding 1: Florida's Foreign National Market Is the Highest-Value Niche

$10.4 billion in international buyer volume, up 51% YoY. 47% pay all-cash. 45% are Latin American. **Converting even 5-10% of cash buyers to leveraged DSCR buyers represents a massive revenue opportunity.** theLender's foreign national program (75% LTV, passport only, FTHB OK) is the best-fit product. Multilingual capabilities (Spanish, Portuguese) are competitive differentiators.

## Finding 2: Insurance Is the Hidden DSCR Killer in Florida

With annual premiums ranging from $2,064 to $9,058 by county, insurance directly impacts whether a property's DSCR ratio is achievable. Inland markets (Jacksonville, Ocala, Port St. Lucie) often produce better DSCR math than coastal markets. **Any lead generation strategy should include insurance-aware property analysis as a value-add.**

## Finding 3: theLender Has the Widest Product Set for Edge Cases

Their foreign national, Section 8, no-ratio (SUPERNONI), AirDNA STR, and non-warrantable condo capabilities cover ICP segments that other lenders cannot serve. **For complex borrower profiles, theLender should be the primary wholesale lender recommendation.**

## Finding 4: The BRRRR-to-DSCR Pipeline Is a Lead Gen Engine

Every hard money/bridge loan that funds a rehab project creates a future DSCR refinance opportunity. **Building relationships with hard money lenders, wholesalers, and rehab contractors creates a self-renewing lead pipeline.**

## Finding 5: 100% Bonus Depreciation Is an Accelerant

Permanently restored in July 2025, 100% bonus depreciation combined with cost segregation studies makes rental property acquisition significantly more attractive for high-income W-2 earners and business owners. This creates a new wave of tax-motivated investors who need DSCR financing.

## Finding 6: Jacksonville Is the Optimal Florida Market for Yield-Focused DSCR

Best rent-to-price ratio (6.8%), positive rent growth, most affordable entry point (~$282K median), crossed 1M population in 2024, strong port/logistics-driven job growth. **For long-term rental investors focused on cash flow, Jacksonville should be the primary market recommendation.**

## Finding 7: The Condo Crisis Creates Niche Opportunity

Distressed condo pricing from post-Surfside compliance costs creates entry points, but most lenders won't finance non-warrantable condos. **The few DSCR lenders that do (theLender, select others) have a captive market for these deals.**

## Finding 8: Self-Employed Borrowers Are the Broadest Addressable Market

This segment overlaps with nearly every other ICP category. The fundamental DSCR value proposition — qualifying on property income rather than tax returns — resonates most powerfully with the millions of business owners whose returns don't reflect their actual financial position.
